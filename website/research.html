About Me
I am a MIT EECS PhD student in CSAIL in applied machine learning. I am interested in using real-world data to build useful technologies and understand natural and human-created systems. To address problems in novel ways, my research deliberately covers a range of domains, including speech recognition and natural language processing, user activity prediction, assistive technology, and public policy. I am passionate about interdisciplinary collaboration and communicating the results to diverse audiences.

Previously, I earned master's degrees at MIT in computer science and the Technology and Policy Program. For my master's thesis, I worked on spoken dialog systems and explored issues of privacy and informed consent, particularly for people who may be experiencing cognitive decline. I was fortunate to spend time at The Boston Home, a specialized-care residence for adults with multiple sclerosis and other progressive neurological diseases.

As an undergraduate, I majored in biomedical engineering in the Engineering Science program at the University of Toronto.
